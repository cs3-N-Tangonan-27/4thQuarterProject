"UNLOCKING NATURE POTENTIAL"

Biotechnology is a multidisciplinary field that merges biological sciences with engineering and technology to create innovative solutions that improve human health, enhance agricultural productivity, and protect the environment. By utilizing living organisms, cells, and biological systems, biotechnology develops products and technologies that address critical challenges and foster sustainable growth. This dynamic field continuously evolves, driven by scientific discovery and technological advancements.

History of Biotechnology:
From ancient practices like fermentation to modern genetic engineering, biotechnology has a rich history of harnessing biological processes for human benefit. Early civilizations used microbial fermentation to produce bread, beer, and wine, laying the foundation for today's advanced biotechnological applications.

Key Concepts:
Genetic modification involves altering the genetic material of organisms to achieve desired traits. Recombinant DNA technology combines DNA from different organisms to create new genetic combinations. Bioinformatics uses computational tools to analyze and interpret biological data, driving innovation and discovery.
